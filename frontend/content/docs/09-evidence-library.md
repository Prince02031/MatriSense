# Evidence Library & Public Sources

### Clinical Disclaimer

> **Clinical Grounding & Accountability**
> *These references informed MatriSense’s conservative triage and guideline-grounded guidance design. MatriSense is not clinically validated, does not diagnose disease, and does not replace doctors or health workers.*

To keep the system safe and transparent:
*   **RAG does not decide urgency:** Retrieval supports guidance, not risk classification.
*   **Rule engine controls risk level:** Confirmed symptoms and profile context are processed by deterministic triage rules.
*   **Human in the loop:** Health workers and registered doctors make final clinical and referral decisions.
*   **Demo Data Notice:** The demo uses synthetic or test data unless deployed under approved governance.

### What This Library Contains
The Evidence Library should include public maternal health references, local Bangladesh resources where available, and internal MatriSense summaries used for rule-aware RAG, danger-sign mapping, safety boundaries, and referral workflow design.

### Suggested Source Groups
*   Maternal warning signs and urgent escalation guidance.
*   Antenatal care and pregnancy danger-sign guidance.
*   High-risk pregnancy references.
*   Bangladesh DGHS, MNCH, or maternal/newborn health resources when available.
*   MatriSense internal knowledge card summaries.
*   MatriSense safety guardrail and fallback template summaries.
*   Regional referral and profile/document feature specifications.

### How Judges Should Read This Section
These sources show why MatriSense uses conservative escalation rules and restricted patient guidance. They do not mean the prototype is clinically validated. Clinical review, field testing, and deployment approvals are required before real-world medical use.
